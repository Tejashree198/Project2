Welcome to Dwarka

Dwarka is believed to have been the first capital of Gujarat

History of Dwarka

The city’s name literally means the “gateway to heaven” in Sanskrit, as Dwar means “gate” and ka references “Brahma”.Dwarka has also been referred to throughout its history as “Mokshapuri”, “Dwarkamati”, and “Dwarkavati”. It is mentioned in the ancient prehistoric epic period of the Mahabharata. According to legend, Krishna settled here after he defeated and killed his uncle Kansa at Mathura.

Famous Places to Visit

Dwarkadhish Temple

Having a history of over 2500 years, this Chalukya style of architecture is a temple dedicated to Lord Krishna.

Nageshwar Temple

One of the 12 famous self-existent temples, Nageshwara Jyotirlinga Temple too houses a curious myth about its origin. The giant, beautiful and artistic statue of Lord Shiva

Rukmini Devi Temple

A small structure is an important shrine dedicated to Krishna's beloved consort, Rukmani Devi. Not as majestically gigantic , this shrine is an architectural masterpiece in itself.

Dwarka Lighthouse

The tower, 43m in height was inaugurated on 15th July 1962. Tourists can enjoy the panoramic view of the sunset.

Dwarka Beach

Along the Arabian Sea coast, Dwarka Beach is a good place to relax in the evenings. Popular among both the locals and tourists, Dwarka Beach is located quite close to the main temples in town.

Local Cuisine

Khaman Dhokhla

Let's kick off our culinary adventure with the iconic Khaman Dhokla. These fluffy, steamed cakes are made from fermented chickpea flour batter, giving them a tangy flavor that is both refreshing and satisfying.

Jalebi Fafda

No trip to Dwarka is complete without indulging in the classic combination of Jalebi and Fafda. Crisp, golden Fafdas paired with syrupy, spiral-shaped Jalebis create a symphony of textures and tastes. Dip the Fafda in green chutney or sauce for an explosion of flavors that will leave your taste buds tingling.

Undhiyo

A hearty and wholesome Gujarati specialty, Undhiyu is a mixed vegetable dish simmered in a fragrant blend of spices and fresh herbs. this flavorful concoction showcases the diverse array of vegetables found in Gujarat's rich agricultural landscape. Served with puris or rotis.

MethinaGota

Thin, crispy, and incredibly versatile, methinagota is a popular snack that is enjoyed by locals and visitors alike. Made from whole wheat flour and seasoned with spices like cumin and ajwai, its a guilt-free indulgence that is perfect for munching on the go. Pair it with a yogurt or a pickle.

Fansi nu Shak

Famous breakfast combination of dwarka gujraat is Fansi nu Shak. One cannot leave gujarat without having it .The dish is super tasty crunchy and mouth watering
