Also known as Kashi is one of the oldest living cities in the world.

History of Varanasi

Abode of Lord Shiva and Parvati, the origins of Varanasi are yet unknown. Ganges in Varanasi is believed to have the power to wash away the sins of mortals. Hindus believe that one who is graced to die on the land of Varanasi would attain salvation and freedom from the cycle of birth and re-birth.

Famous Places to Visit

Ganga Arti

The Ganga Aarti at Varanasi is a mesmerizing ritual every evening at Dashashwamedh Ghat, among other locations along the river. This ceremonial worship of the Ganges involves priests offering multi-tiered oil lamps, accompanied by rhythmic chants bells.

Tebetian Temple

Varanasi's Tibetan Temple stands as a symbol of peace and serenity, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of Tibet. This temple's traditional Tibetan architecture, including vibrant prayer flags and a large prayer wheel, offers a unique spiritual experience.

Shri kashivishvanath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India The Shri Kashi Vishwanath Temple is a beacon of faith and spirituality in Varanasi, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Housing one of the twelve Jyotirlingas attracts millions of devotees year-round, seeking blessings and spiritual fulfilment.

DhameK Stupa

The Dhamek Stupa, located in Sarnath, is a significant monument marking the spot where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon to his five disciples after enlightenment.

Assi ghat

Assi Ghat, where the Assi River meets the Ganges, is a vibrant cultural hub and one of Varanasi's most popular ghats. It is the starting point for pilgrimages and is famous for its morning and evening rituals

Sarnath Temple

Sarnath is where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon after enlightenment, marking Buddhism's foundation. This tranquil site hosts the Dhamek Stupa, ancient monasteries

Local Cuisine

Banarsi Paan

“khai ke paan banaras wala, khul jaaye band akal ka taala”. Say Banaras and the first thing that comes to mind is Banarasi paan, As per locals, your meal is incomplete if you do not have a paan at the end of having the best Local Food Varanasi.

Laal Peda

This milk dish isn’t new to us as we also prepare a similar dish in my home state including our best local food in Varanasi.

Litti Chokha

litti chokha the best street Food in Varanasi, is a tasty snack that you can enjoy at different street food joints and eateries around the city. This famous Banaras food looks very similar to the dal bati of Rajasthan Anand.

Malaiyo

A delicacy in Varanasi, malaiyyo is a melt-in-the-mouth sweet dish which is available only in winter and among best local food in Varanasi. It is made of milk foam and the preparation process is quite special.

Launglata

FLaunglata is a sweet dish that the locals mostly love to eat during Holi Celebrations. However, it is available in the sweet shops and street food stalls, as the best local food in Varanasi all year.
