History of Mathura

Mathura, which lies at the centre of the cultural region of Braj has an ancient history and is also believed to be the homeland and birthplace of Krishna, who belonged to the Yadu dynasty. According to the Archaeological Survey of India plaque at the Mathura Museum,[9] the city is mentioned in the oldest Indian epic, the Ramayana. In the epic, the Ikshwaku prince Shatrughna slays a demon called Lavanasura and claims the land. Afterwards, the place came to be known as Madhuvan as it was thickly wooded, then Madhupura and later Mathura.

Famous Places to Visit

shri KRishna Janmabhumi Temple

It is believed that this temple is situated at the place where Lord Krishna was born and the result of many excavations has tried to prove this belief.

Shri Dwarkadish Temple

The Dwarkadhish Temple, touted as one of the holiest temples in Mathura, is famous all over the country for its elaborate architecture and paintings. Built in 1814, the temple is relatively new but is highly revered.

Raman Reti Gokhul

This incredible place called Raman Van or Raman Reti―whose sacred sands (reti) are redolent with the stories of a bygone era when Lord Krishna frequented to engage in divine plays (raman) with his brother, Balarama and his cowherd friends.

Aloo Kachori

Aloo Kachori is a beloved street food in Mathura. It consists of a crispy, deep-fried pastry shell filled with a spicy and tangy potato mixture.

Mathura's pedas are not only the best food in Mathura but are also known across the nation. They are delicious and deeply ingrained in the city's culinary tradition.
