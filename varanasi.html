History of Varanasi

Varansi, one of the most ancient cities of India, is located on the eastern bank of the Kshipra River. Known as Avantika in ancient times, it was one of the primary cities where students went to study during the Mauryan empire.

Famous Places to Visit

Ganga Arti

The Ganga Aarti at Varanasi is a mesmerizing ritual every evening at Dashashwamedh Ghat, among other locations along the river. This ceremonial worship of the Ganges involves priests using fire as an offering in the form of multi-tiered oil lamps, accompanied by rhythmic chants and ringing bells.

Tebetian Temple

Varanasi's Tibetan Temple stands as a symbol of peace and serenity, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of Tibet. This temple's traditional Tibetan architecture, including vibrant prayer flags and a large prayer wheel, offers a unique spiritual experience.
